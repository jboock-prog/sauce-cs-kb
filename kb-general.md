## Entry GEN-1: Adding a Contact in HubSpot

**Title:** Adding a New Contact in HubSpot
**Issue Type:** Policies & Rules
**Situation:** An agent needs to create or link a contact in HubSpot — for an end customer, restaurant staff, or any new contact encountered on a ticket.
**Resolution:**

**From the Contacts Page:**
1. Go to the HubSpot Contacts Page.
2. Click "Create contact" in the top right.
3. Fill in as much information as available. For end customers, always add both email address and phone number.

**From a specific Ticket or Company:**
1. On the right side of the ticket, find the Contacts tab.
2. Click Add → Create new → fill in the information. For end customers, always add both email and phone number.

**If you get a "contact already exists" error:**
- Switch to "Add existing" and link the contact that already exists instead of creating a duplicate.

**To edit missing or incorrect contact information:**
- Select "Preview" on the contact and update as needed.

**Exceptions:** Never create a duplicate contact — always check for an existing record first.
**Approval Required:** No.

## Entry GEN-3: UCC Emails

**Title:** Handling UCC (Uniform Commercial Code) Emails
**Issue Type:** Policies & Rules
**Situation:** A UCC email arrives in the inbox.
**Resolution:**
1. Do **not** reply to the email.
2. Forward it to: **ucc@getsauce.com**
3. Remove the contact from the ticket.
4. Close the ticket.

**Exceptions:** None — no response, no engagement, just forward and close.

## Entry GEN-4: Handling Spam Emails

**Title:** Spam Email Handling — What to Act On vs. What to Close
**Issue Type:** Policies & Rules
**Situation:** An email lands in the spam folder. Agent needs to determine whether to dismiss it or treat it as a real ticket.
**Resolution:**
To view the spam folder: HubSpot Inbox → "More" tab → Spam.

**If the email is spam (not relevant):** Close the conversation. No ticket needed.

**If the email is relevant:** Click "Mark as not spam" — it will move to Unassigned and auto-create a ticket. Work it as a normal ticket.

**Spam vs. Not Spam classification:**

| Email Type | Classification | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Advertisements | Spam | Close |
| Out of office notices | Spam | Close |
| Mailer Daemon (failed send) | Spam — but check which email failed; likely relevant to an existing ticket | Investigate then close |
| Sauce Orders | Not spam | Book with Dlivrd |
| Disable dashboard requests | Not spam | Disable store as requested |

**Exceptions:** Mailer Daemon emails look like spam but often indicate a delivery failure for a ticket-related email — always check before closing.
**Approval Required:** No.

## Entry GEN-5: Handling Missed Calls (After Hours)

**Title:** Missed Calls Received During Closed Hours
**Issue Type:** Policies & Rules
**Situation:** A customer (B2C or B2B) called during closed hours (1 AM – 8 AM EST). They heard the voicemail and received an SMS. When CS reopens, the missed call appears as a ticket.
**Resolution:**
1. Check if an existing ticket already covers this customer's issue — if yes, reply within that ticket and merge the missed call ticket into it.
2. If no existing ticket: call the customer back and assist them.
3. If the customer doesn't answer: send an email and/or SMS to follow up.

**Closed hours voicemail/SMS message:**
> "Hello, we are currently closed. Our hours of operation are from 8 AM to 1 AM EST, Monday through Sunday. Please call us back during business hours, or email us at support@getsauce.com and we will get back to you as soon as we can."

**Exceptions:** None.
**Approval Required:** No.

## Entry GEN-6: OB Request to Connect a Website Domain

**Title:** Onboarding Team Request to Connect a Website Domain
**Issue Type:** Policies & Rules
**Situation:** An email arrives from an Onboarding agent asking to connect a restaurant's domain. This is a BO task, not a Support task.
**Resolution:**
1. Reply to the email confirming the request has been received.
2. Close the conversation.
3. Create a ticket to BO with the appropriate category.
4. Attach the company to the ticket on the right side of the interface.
   - If the store name is in the email body: use it.
   - If the store name is only in the subject line: use that.
   - If the store name is not mentioned anywhere: ask OB to clarify before proceeding.

**Exceptions:** Never attempt to connect a domain directly — always route to BO.
**Approval Required:** No — route to BO.
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-25 — extracted from Playbook

## Entry GEN-7: Change of Date on a Shelsky's Order

**Title:** Shelsky's Catering Order — Change the Order Date
**Issue Type:** Order Issues
**Situation:** A date change is needed on a Shelsky's catering order.
**Resolution:**
1. Get confirmation from the Shelsky's store that they are aware of the date change and can accommodate the new time.
2. Ask Tier 2 to update the date displayed on the dashboard.
3. Once updated, the store will see the correct time on their tablet and the receipt will print at the new time.

**Exceptions:** Date changes require store confirmation before Tier 2 makes the update.
**Approval Required:** Yes — store must confirm before the date is changed.

## Entry GEN-8: Calls About PDF Subscriptions (Wrong Number)

**Title:** Incoming Calls About PDF Services / Doctor's Note Subscriptions (Wrong Number)
**Issue Type:** Policies & Rules
**Situation:** A caller is trying to cancel a subscription for a PDF service or doctor's note — they've dialed Sauce's number by mistake.
**Resolution:**
1. Politely explain that they've reached the wrong company: "We are a company that works with restaurants and their online orders — we're not the service you're looking for."
2. If a ticket was opened for the call, close it using the category **"Other."**

**Exceptions:** None — do not attempt to help with the PDF service request.
**Approval Required:** No.
